# California — Unclaimed Property Claim Requirements (Demonstration)

> **For demonstration only — not legal advice.** This document is synthetic and does not
> reflect the actual rules of the California State Controller's Office or any real program.

## Overview

To recover escheated property in California, a claimant must prove identity, prove their
connection to the reported owner and address, and satisfy notarization and (where applicable)
deceased-owner or business-entity requirements.

## Proof of Identity

A current government-issued photo ID is required: a state driver's license, state ID card, or
US passport. If no photo ID is available, the claimant may submit two alternates from: Social
Security card, recent utility bill, or voter registration. Identity documents must show a name
that matches the reported owner or a documented prior name.

## Proof of Address

Provide one proof of current address dated within the last 60 days (utility bill, bank
statement, or lease). When the claimant's current address differs from the property's last
known address, also provide a document linking the claimant to the reported address, or a
name-change record (e.g., marriage certificate) where the name differs.

## Notarization

Claims with a total value **over $1,000** require a **notarized** claim form. Claims of $1,000
or less may be submitted with an unnotarized signed claim form.

## Deceased Owner / Heir Claims

If the reported owner is deceased, provide a certified death certificate plus proof of the
claimant's right to claim: an Affidavit for Collection of Personal Property for small estates,
or Letters of Administration / Letters Testamentary for estates administered through probate.

## Business Entity Claims

A business claimant must provide Articles of Incorporation or Organization, proof of EIN (IRS
confirmation letter), and a corporate resolution or authorization identifying the authorized
signer.

## Processing Times

Standard processing is 30–60 days after a complete claim package is received. Claims requiring
heir or business documentation may take longer.
