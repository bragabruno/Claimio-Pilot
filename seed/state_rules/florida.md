# Florida — Unclaimed Property Claim Requirements (Demonstration)

> **For demonstration only — not legal advice.** This document is synthetic and does not
> reflect the actual rules of the Florida Department of Financial Services or any real program.

## Overview

Florida applies a strict identity standard and a low notarization threshold, so most claims
require a notarized form.

## Proof of Identity

A government-issued photo ID is required for all claims (Florida driver's license, Florida ID
card, or US passport). Alternate-document substitution is not accepted; a photo ID must be
provided.

## Proof of Address

Provide a current proof of address. Where the claimant's address differs from the reported
address, include a document linking the claimant to the reported address or a recorded name
change.

## Notarization

Claims **over $500** require a **notarized** claim form. Because the threshold is low, the
majority of claims will require notarization. Claims of $500 or less may use a signed form.

## Deceased Owner / Heir Claims

For a deceased owner, provide a certified death certificate plus Letters of Administration. For
small estates, an affidavit of heirs may be accepted in lieu of letters, accompanied by the
death certificate.

## Business Entity Claims

A business claimant must provide Articles of Incorporation or Organization, proof of EIN, and a
corporate authorization identifying the authorized signer.

## Processing Times

Standard processing is 30–90 days after a complete claim package is received.
