# Illinois — Unclaimed Property Claim Requirements (Demonstration)

> **For demonstration only — not legal advice.** This document is synthetic and does not
> reflect the actual rules of the Illinois State Treasurer or any real program.

## Overview

Illinois supports an expedited electronic claim path for lower-value claims and uses a
small-estate affidavit pathway for deceased owners.

## Proof of Identity

Provide a government-issued photo ID (Illinois driver's license, Illinois ID card, or US
passport). The name must match the reported owner or a documented prior name.

## Proof of Address

Provide a current proof of address. If the reported address differs, include a document linking
the claimant to the reported address or a name-change record.

## Notarization

Claims **over $1,500** require a **notarized** claim form. Claims of $1,500 or less may be filed
electronically with a signed (unnotarized) attestation.

## Deceased Owner / Heir Claims

For a deceased owner, provide a certified death certificate plus, for estates under $100,000, a
Small Estate Affidavit. For larger estates, provide Letters of Office issued by the probate
court.

## Business Entity Claims

A business claimant must provide Articles of Incorporation or Organization, proof of EIN, and a
corporate resolution naming the authorized signer.

## Processing Times

Standard processing is 60–120 days. Electronic claims under the notarization threshold are
typically processed faster.
