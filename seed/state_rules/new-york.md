# New York — Unclaimed Property Claim Requirements (Demonstration)

> **For demonstration only — not legal advice.** This document is synthetic and does not
> reflect the actual rules of the New York State Office of the Comptroller or any real program.

## Overview

New York requires proof of identity, a documented link to the reported owner and address, and
escalating verification for higher-value claims.

## Proof of Identity

Provide a government-issued photo ID (driver's license, non-driver ID, or US passport). The
signature on the claim form must be consistent with the signature on the submitted ID.

## Proof of Address

Provide documentation linking the claimant to the address reported by the holder. If the
claimant has moved, include a prior-address proof (old utility bill, tax record) or a forwarding
record connecting the current and reported addresses.

## Notarization

Claims **over $2,500** require a **notarized** claim form. Claims **over $10,000** additionally
require a **medallion signature guarantee** from a participating financial institution. Claims of
$2,500 or less may be submitted with a signed (unnotarized) form.

## Deceased Owner / Heir Claims

For a deceased owner, provide a certified death certificate and Letters Testamentary or Letters
of Administration. For small estates under $50,000, a Voluntary Administration (small-estate)
affidavit may be accepted in place of full letters.

## Business Entity Claims

A business claimant must provide a Certificate of Incorporation (or equivalent formation
document), proof of EIN, and a corporate resolution authorizing the signer to claim on behalf
of the entity.

## Processing Times

Standard processing is approximately 90 days after a complete package is received. Medallion-
guaranteed and heir claims may require additional review time.
