# Texas — Unclaimed Property Claim Requirements (Demonstration)

> **For demonstration only — not legal advice.** This document is synthetic and does not
> reflect the actual rules of the Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts or any real program.

## Overview

Texas requires proof of identity, proof of the claimant's connection to the reported owner and
address, and uses an heirship affidavit pathway for deceased owners.

## Proof of Identity

Provide a government-issued photo ID (Texas driver's license, Texas ID card, or US passport).
The name must match the reported owner or a documented former name.

## Proof of Address

Provide a current proof of address and, where the reported address differs, a document
establishing the historical link to the reported address (prior lease, utility record, or tax
document).

## Notarization

Claims **over $5,000** require a **notarized** claim form. Claims of $5,000 or less may be
submitted with a signed (unnotarized) form.

## Deceased Owner / Heir Claims

For a deceased owner, provide a certified death certificate plus either a Texas **Affidavit of
Heirship** (for property passing outside probate) or a Small Estate Affidavit. If the estate was
probated, Letters Testamentary may be substituted.

## Business Entity Claims

A business claimant must provide a Certificate of Formation, proof of EIN, and documentation of
the authorized signer (corporate resolution or equivalent).

## Processing Times

Standard processing is 60–90 days after a complete claim package is received.
